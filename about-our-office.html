Michael W. Bell, DDS, MAGD, LLSR

Dr. Bell is a native Texan. He earned his undergraduate degree from Texas A&M University and then went on to earn his DDS with honors from Baylor College of Dentistry.

Mastership Academy of General Dentistry (MAGD) 

After years of continuing education and clinical testing, Dr. Bell obtained his Mastership in the Academy of General Dentistry. To earn this honor, he spent his time studying every discipline in the field of dentistry. This unique perspective allows him to better serve his patients.

“Achieving Mastership is a professional designation within the Academy of General Dentistry beyond FAGD, and reflects a general dentist’s ongoing commitment to provide quality care through continuing education.”

Approximately 1,800 of the 37,000 members of the Academy of General Dentistry have gone on to receive their MAGD. It is the highest distinction a member of the AGD can receive. The requirements are as follows:

  • Attain Fellowship status in the Academy of General Dentistry
  • Earn an extra 600 approved continuing education credits, meeting minimum requirements in certain dental disciplines
  • Of those hours, 400 must be hands-on courses In total, a Master of the Academy of General Dentistry has taken a total of 1,100 hours of continuing education and studied 16 disciplines in dentistry.

Lifelong Learning & Service Recognition (LLSR)

The Academy of General Dentistry (AGD), a professional association of more than 37,000 general dentists dedicated to providing quality dental care and oral health education to the public, is pleased to announce that Michael W. Bell DDS, of Bedford, Texas has earned the 2012 Lifelong Learning & Service Recognition (LLSR) for his commitment to lifelong learning, volunteering his services to communities in need, mentoring associates and new dentists, and participating in organized dentistry.

The LLSR is presented to AGD members who have gone above and beyond earning the association’s highest honor of Mastership. To accomplish this recognition, Dr. Bell completed at least 1,600 hours of continuing dental education in 16 different dental disciplines. In addition, recipients must complete at least 100 hours of dental-related community or volunteer service.

“Recipients of the LLSR are true leaders among our organization and wholly embrace the AGD’s core principles and ideals,” says AGD President Jeffrey M. Cole, DDS, MBA, FAGD. “For Dr. Bell this award symbolizes a career of excellence in dentistry and demonstrates the characteristics of a role model to his fellow dentists and to members of the community.”

Since its inception in 2005, only 189 of the AGD’s 37,000-plus members have received the prestigious LLSR.
